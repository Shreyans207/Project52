  • Chameleons are a distinctive Old World lizards with 202 species.
  • Skin Crystals enable them to change color.
  • Chameleons have eyes in the backs of their heads. A Chameleon's eyes can swivel 180 degrees.
  • Excluding their tail, some chameleons’ tongues can be twice the length of their body. Their tongues consist of both muscles and bones, and can bend round to grab their pray.




  • Camels can completely shut their nostrils during sandstorms. They have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  • Thick pads of skin on their chest and knees, camels can comfortably sit in very hot sand. Camels can travel at up to 40 miles per hour
  • Their humps let them store up to 80 pounds of fat which they can live off for weeks and even months!! When a camel finally does find water, he can drink up to 40 gallons in one go.
  • Camels are very strong and can carry up to 900 pounds for 25 miles a day.




  • An adult panda weight more than 45 kilos (100 pounds), and can be about 1.5 meters (5 feet) long! An adult can eat 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day!
  • Pandas are "lazy" — eating and sleeping make their day. Pandas have carnivorous teeth, but they eat bamboo and fruit!
  • Giant pandas are good at climbing trees and can also swim. They can climb trees from 7 months old.




  • Bears are extraordinarily intelligent animals. They have far superior navigation skills to humans; excellent memories; large brain to body ratio!!
  • Bears have excellent senses of smell, sight and hearing. They can smell food, cubs, a mate or predators from miles away. Their great eyesight allows them to detect when fruits are ripe.
  • Bears care deeply about family members. They will risk their lives and even fight to the death in order to save a cub or sibling from danger.
  • The bear is a common national personification for Russia (and the former USSR) and Germany. The brown bear is Finland’s national animal.




  • Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals. They are one of only a small number of species to pass the ‘mirror test’ – a test of self recognition
  • They can fly at altitudes up to and beyond 6000 feet, and at an average speed of 77.6 mph. They are renowned for their outstanding navigational abilities using a range of skills.
  • Pigeons have excellent hearing abilities. They can detect sounds at far low frequencies and can thus hear distant storms and volcanoes. They are actually very clean animals.
  • Both female and male pigeons share responsibility of caring for and raising young. They and humans have lived in close proximity for thousands of years




  • There are around 3,000 cicada species, so they vary in size from 2.2 to 5.5 cm long. Their wings are transparent and can seen with a light source. Each species of cicada has a different song.
  • Cicadas can live a relatively long time : 4 to 17 years. They spend most of their lives underground as larvae, followed by a short adulthood — from two to six weeks — above ground.
  • Tens of thousands to over a million insects can reside in a relatively small area of land. A group of periodicals that emerge at the same time is called a brood.
  • Other Facts :- Cicadas are herbivores. Young cicadas eat liquid from plant roots. Adult cicadas do not feed. Cicadas do not sting or bite. When they leave their burrow, sometimes cicadas will make chimneys of mud over the exit.
